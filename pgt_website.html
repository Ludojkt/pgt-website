About PGT

A World-Class Joint Venture for Indonesia's Maritime Future

Three global and regional leaders combining port expertise, industrial capabilities, and deep knowledge of Indonesia's logistics landscape.

Patimban Global Gateway Terminal (PGT) is a deep-sea container terminal operator established to elevate Indonesia's maritime capabilities. Located approximately three hours east of Jakarta in Subang Regency, PGT operates as one of Indonesia's National Strategic Projects, designed to support national supply chains and reduce overcapacity at Tanjung Priok.

Supported by the Government of Japan through JICA financing, PGT stands at the intersection of global expertise and local ambition.

PGT is a long-term, 37-year sub-concessionaire entrusted with developing and operating the container terminal of Patimban International Port, awarded by PT Pelabuhan Patimban Internasional (PPI).

Strategic Location

At the Crossroads of West Java's Industrial Belt

Positioned between Karawang and Subang industrial estates — Indonesia's fastest-growing manufacturing corridor.

Location Advantages

Why Patimban

A strategic alternative to congested Jakarta ports, offering faster access to West Java's industrial heartland.

Shorter Transit Times

Only 1.5–2 hours from Karawang by truck (vs. 4–6 hours to Tanjung Priok through Jakarta congestion).

Toll Road Connectivity

Direct access to Trans-Java corridor via the upcoming Patimban Toll Road (2026) — just 30 minutes from the main highway.

Zero Congestion

No congestion on approach roads or inside the port — unlike Jakarta's heavily saturated corridors.

Industrial Proximity

Close to automotive (Toyota, Honda, Daihatsu), electronics, FMCG, and manufacturing clusters across Karawang and Subang.
